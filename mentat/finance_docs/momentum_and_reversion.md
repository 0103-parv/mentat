# Momentum and mean reversion

Momentum is the tendency of assets that have risen (fallen) recently to keep rising
(falling) over the medium term, typically measured over 3 to 12 months. A momentum
signal goes long recent winners and short recent losers. Momentum has been documented
across many markets but is regime-dependent and suffers sharp crashes after market
rebounds.

Mean reversion is the opposite tendency: prices that moved far from a reference (a moving
average, or simply the last move) tend to revert. Short-horizon (daily) reversal is a
classic mean-reversion effect. Reversion strategies trade against the last move and tend
to have high turnover, so transaction costs matter a lot. Whether momentum or reversion
"works" depends on horizon, asset, regime, and costs — and any apparent edge must be
verified out-of-sample before it is believed.
