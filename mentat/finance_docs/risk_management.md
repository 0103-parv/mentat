# Risk management: drawdown, sizing, diversification

Maximum drawdown is the largest peak-to-trough decline of an equity curve; it measures
how much pain a strategy inflicts before recovering, and it often matters more to
survival than average return because large drawdowns can force deleveraging or
abandonment. Volatility (standard deviation of returns) measures dispersion but is
symmetric; drawdown and downside-deviation focus on the losses that actually hurt.

Position sizing controls risk per bet; a common rule is to scale exposure inversely to
volatility (vol targeting) so risk stays roughly constant. Diversification across
uncorrelated return streams lowers portfolio volatility for a given expected return —
the one "free lunch" in finance — but correlations tend to rise in crises, so
diversification helps least exactly when it is needed most. Leverage amplifies both
returns and drawdowns and introduces the risk of ruin.
