# Factor investing

Factor investing builds portfolios around systematic drivers of return called factors.
The most studied equity factors are: market (beta), size (small-cap premium), value
(cheap vs expensive on book-to-price or similar), momentum (recent winners), quality
(profitable, stable firms), and low-volatility. The Fama-French three-factor model added
size and value to the market factor; later work added profitability and investment
(five-factor model), and momentum is often included separately (Carhart).

Factors are long-short: long the high-exposure names, short the low. Many documented
factors weaken after publication (factor decay / crowding), and apparent factors can be
artifacts of data mining across many candidates. A factor is credible only if it has an
economic rationale and survives out-of-sample, across markets, and after costs — the same
verification discipline that applies to any alpha.
